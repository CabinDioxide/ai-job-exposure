Methodology Comparison: Anthropic vs Karpathy 方法论对比：Anthropic vs Karpathy

This project offers two independent approaches to measuring AI occupational exposure, available as separate visualization layers:

Anthropic Economic Index (Primary — "Theoretical Exposure")

  • Method: Bottom-up task-level analysis. Each of 17,998 O*NET task statements was evaluated for whether Claude can meaningfully assist. The task_penetration.csv file records the penetration rate per task. Each occupation's exposure is the weighted sum of its constituent tasks' penetration rates.
  • Scale: Raw values 0–0.75 (continuous), normalized to 0–10 for display. The maximum raw observed_exposure in job_exposure.csv is 0.7451 (Telemarketers).
  • Coverage: 756 SOC 2018 occupations. Mapped to 417/436 ISCO-08 codes (96% coverage) via crosswalk + 28 manual mappings for critical occupations that changed between SOC versions.
  • Strengths: Grounded in actual task analysis rather than subjective rating. High granularity (task-level, not occupation-level). Consistent methodology across all occupations.
  • Weaknesses: Based on Claude's current capabilities, which may not represent all AI systems. Task penetration is binary (can/cannot assist) rather than measuring degree of assistance.

Karpathy LLM Assessment (Comparison — "Karpathy LLM Score")

  • Method: Top-down LLM judgment. An LLM was asked: "On a scale of 1-10, how much can AI impact this occupation?" with a written rationale for each score.
  • Scale: Integer scores 1–10 with text rationale.
  • Coverage: 290 US SOC occupations (mapped to 174/436 ISCO codes, 40% coverage).
  • Strengths: Intuitive, holistic assessment. Rationale text provides interpretability. Considers factors beyond current tool capabilities (e.g., future AI potential).
  • Weaknesses: Subjective — reflects the LLM's training biases. Systematically overestimates exposure for well-known occupations (avg 5.4/10 vs Anthropic's avg 3.2/10). Lower coverage (40% vs 96% of ISCO codes).

Why include both? The Anthropic method is empirically grounded but limited to what Claude can currently do. The Karpathy method captures broader intuitions about AI's potential impact. Comparing the two reveals where current AI usage lags behind perceived potential — a gap that may close as AI capabilities expand.

Why the Score Gap Is So Large

Across 290 occupations with both scores, the Anthropic mean is 2.1/10 while Karpathy's is 5.4/10. The Pearson correlation is only 0.508. This is not a calibration error — the two methods measure fundamentally different things.

Anthropic's distribution is extremely right-skewed. Of 756 SOC occupations in job_exposure.csv, 411 (54%) score exactly zero, and 72% score below 1.0/10 after normalization. Only 5% exceed 5.0/10. This reflects the narrow range of tasks Claude is currently used for — dominated by software development, data analysis, content writing, and customer service.

Karpathy's distribution is approximately normal. Scores are evenly spread from 1–10, with a mean of 5.4 and median of 6. When an LLM is asked "how much can AI impact this occupation?", it considers theoretical potential, future capabilities, and general knowledge — resulting in moderate-to-high scores for nearly all knowledge work.

Case study: Insurance Underwriters. Karpathy gives 9/10 — AI can clearly analyze risk profiles, process applications, and assess claims. Anthropic gives 0.8/10 — almost nobody is currently using Claude for insurance underwriting. The 8.2-point gap is not wrong; it reveals a massive adoption gap. The AI capability exists but hasn't been deployed at scale in this field.

The largest gaps (Karpathy ≫ Anthropic) cluster in finance, HR, and specialized analysis roles — occupations where AI could help but hasn't penetrated yet. The smallest gaps appear in software development and customer service, where Claude adoption is already high.

  • 劣势：基于Claude当前能力，可能无法代表所有AI系统。任务渗透是二元的（能/不能辅助），而非衡量辅助程度。

  • 方法：自上而下的LLM判断。向LLM提问："在1-10的量表上，AI对这个职业的影响有多大？"并为每个评分附文字理由。
  • 量表：1–10整数评分，附文字理由。
  • 覆盖范围：290个美国SOC职业（映射到174/436个ISCO代码，40%覆盖率）。
  • 优势：直觉性强、整体评估。理由文本提供可解释性。考虑了超越当前工具能力的因素（如未来AI潜力）。
  • 劣势：主观性强——反映LLM的训练偏差。系统性高估知名职业的暴露度（平均5.4/10 vs Anthropic的平均3.2/10）。覆盖率较低（40% vs 96% ISCO代码）。

为什么两种分数差距这么大

在290个同时具有两种分数的职业中，Anthropic均值为2.1/10，Karpathy均值为5.4/10。皮尔逊相关系数仅为0.508。这不是校准错误——两种方法在衡量根本不同的东西。

Anthropic的分布极度右偏。job_exposure.csv中756个SOC职业里，411个（54%）得分为零，归一化后72%低于1.0/10。仅5%超过5.0/10。这反映了Claude当前被使用的任务范围非常狭窄——以软件开发、数据分析、内容写作和客户服务为主。

Karpathy的分布近似正态。分数均匀分布在1–10之间，均值5.4，中位数6。当LLM被问到"AI对这个职业的影响有多大"时，它会考虑理论潜力、未来能力和通用知识——导致几乎所有知识工作都获得中高分。

Data Quality Issues & Biases 数据质量问题与偏差

1. Claude Usage Distribution Bias

The observed exposure data reflects who actually uses Claude, not who could benefit from AI. Claude's user base is heavily concentrated in software development, content writing, and data analysis. This creates systematic blind spots:

  • Overrepresented: Software developers, data scientists, content writers, marketing professionals, researchers — these occupations show high observed exposure partly because their practitioners adopted Claude early.
  • Underrepresented: Healthcare workers, teachers, legal professionals, tradespeople — many of these occupations could benefit from AI but haven't adopted Claude at scale, making their observed exposure artificially low.
  • Geographic bias: English-speaking countries have 5-10x more Claude usage per capita than non-English-speaking countries. India's large sample (58,098 conversations) is almost entirely from IT professionals, not representative of its 560M-person labor force.

2. SOC Version Mismatch

O*NET task statements use SOC 2010 codes; Anthropic's job_exposure.csv uses SOC 2018 codes. Between these versions, major occupations were restructured:

  • Software Developers split from 15-1131/15-1132 (2010) into 15-1252/15-1253/15-1254 (2018)
  • Web Developers moved from 15-1134 to 15-1254
  • Database Administrators split into 15-1242/15-1243/15-1244

We mitigate this with 28 manual mappings for critical occupations, but edge cases remain.

3. SOC-to-ISCO Mapping Loss

The SOC (US) to ISCO-08 (international) crosswalk is many-to-many. When multiple SOC codes map to one ISCO code, we average their scores. When one SOC maps to multiple ISCO codes, all receive the same score. This averaging smooths out meaningful within-group variation.

4. Employment & Pay Data: US vs Non-US

The employment data quality differs drastically between the US and all other countries:

  • US: Employment counts come from BLS Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS), with individual counts for each of 290 SOC occupations. This is census-grade data — each tile in the treemap reflects the actual number of workers in that specific job.
  • Non-US: We use ILOSTAT data, which only reports employment at the ISCO major group level (10 categories: Managers, Professionals, Technicians, etc.). Within each major group, employment is divided equally among all 4-digit ISCO occupations in that group. This means a "Software Developer" and a "Mathematician" both get the same employment count if they're in the same major group — a significant distortion.

Concretely: If ILOSTAT says the UK has 5.4 million "Professionals" and there are 90 ISCO codes in that group, each occupation gets 60,000 workers. This is mathematically necessary but misleading — there are far more teachers than astronomers.

5. Pay Data Gaps & Currency Issues

ILOSTAT earnings data is available for 9 of 12 countries. Canada, Australia, and Japan lack ILOSTAT earnings coverage entirely. For countries with earnings data, the ILOSTAT unit scale varies — Korea reports in thousands of KRW, India needs a ×100 multiplier, Mexico needs ×10, Philippines needs ×100. We apply per-country unit corrections but some inaccuracies may remain. Like employment, pay data is only available at the ISCO major group level, so all occupations within the same group show identical salary.

6. India & Philippines: Informal Economy Blind Spots

India and the Philippines present unique data quality challenges that go beyond sparse coverage:

  • India: ILO estimates 89% of India's workforce is in informal employment — street vendors, agricultural day laborers, domestic workers, construction workers. ILOSTAT employment statistics primarily capture the formal sector. India's 482 million "jobs" in our dataset are a mix of formal counts and modeled estimates. The 5.6 million in "Professionals" largely reflects IT and engineering — not because India lacks other professionals, but because those are the occupations ILOSTAT can count. Meanwhile, Claude usage data (58,098 conversations) comes almost entirely from the IT sector in Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Pune. The result: India's data tells a coherent story about its tech sector, but is essentially silent on 90% of its economy.
  • Philippines: Similar issues at smaller scale. 55 ISCO codes with non-zero observed exposure (vs 144 for the UK). The BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) sector — call centers, data entry — dominates Claude usage, creating a lopsided view. The Philippines' 46.9 million jobs include large agricultural and informal service sectors invisible to both ILOSTAT granular data and Claude usage patterns.

7. Temporal Snapshot

V4 data covers a single week (Nov 13–20, 2025). AI adoption patterns evolve rapidly. A company rolling out Claude enterprise during the sampling week could significantly skew a small country's data. The theoretical exposure (Anthropic task analysis) is also a point-in-time snapshot of Claude's capabilities as of early 2026.

1. Claude使用分布偏差

实测暴露度数据反映的是谁在实际使用Claude，而非谁能从AI中受益。Claude的用户群高度集中在软件开发、内容写作和数据分析领域。这造成了系统性盲区：

  • 过度代表：软件开发人员、数据科学家、内容写手、市场营销人员、研究人员——这些职业显示出高实测暴露度，部分原因是其从业者较早采用了Claude。
  • 代表不足：医护人员、教师、法律专业人士、技工——这些职业中许多可以从AI中受益，但尚未大规模采用Claude，导致其实测暴露度人为偏低。
  • 地理偏差：英语国家的人均Claude使用量是非英语国家的5-10倍。印度的大样本（58,098次对话）几乎全部来自IT从业者，无法代表其5.6亿劳动力。

2. SOC版本错位

O*NET任务描述使用SOC 2010代码；Anthropic的job_exposure.csv使用SOC 2018代码。两个版本之间，主要职业经历了重组：

  • 软件开发人员从15-1131/15-1132（2010）拆分为15-1252/15-1253/15-1254（2018）
  • 网页开发人员从15-1134移至15-1254
  • 数据库管理员拆分为15-1242/15-1243/15-1244

我们通过28个关键职业的手动映射来缓解这个问题，但仍存在边界情况。

3. SOC到ISCO映射损失

SOC（美国）到ISCO-08（国际）的交叉映射是多对多关系。当多个SOC代码映射到一个ISCO代码时，我们取平均值。当一个SOC映射到多个ISCO代码时，所有代码获得相同分数。这种平均化消除了组内的有意义差异。

4. 就业数据：美国 vs 非美国

美国与其他国家的就业数据质量存在巨大差异：

  • 美国：就业人数来自BLS职业就业与工资统计（OEWS），为290个SOC职业各自提供独立计数。这是普查级数据——树状图中每个色块反映的是该具体职业的实际工人数量。
  • 非美国：我们使用ILOSTAT数据，仅报告ISCO大类级别的就业（10个类别：管理人员、专业人员、技术人员等）。在每个大类内部，就业人数在该组所有4位ISCO职业之间均等分配。这意味着"软件开发人员"和"数学家"如果在同一大类，获得的就业人数完全相同——这是显著的失真。

具体来说：如果ILOSTAT显示英国有540万"专业人员"，该组有90个ISCO代码，则每个职业分配到6万人。这在数学上是必要的，但会产生误导——教师的数量远多于天文学家。

5. 薪资数据缺口与货币问题

ILOSTAT收入数据覆盖12个国家中的9个。加拿大、澳大利亚和日本完全缺乏ILOSTAT收入数据。对于有收入数据的国家，ILOSTAT的单位标度各不相同——韩国以千韩元为单位，印度需要×100倍数，墨西哥需要×10，菲律宾需要×100。我们逐国应用了单位校正，但仍可能存在不准确之处。与就业数据一样，薪资数据也仅在ISCO大类级别可用，因此同一组内所有职业显示相同的薪资水平。

6. 印度与菲律宾：非正规经济盲区

印度和菲律宾面临超越数据稀疏性的独特数据质量挑战：

  • 印度：ILO估计印度89%的劳动力从事非正规就业——街头摊贩、农业日工、家政工人、建筑工人。ILOSTAT就业统计主要捕获正规部门。我们数据集中印度的4.82亿"岗位"是正规统计和模型估算的混合体。"专业人员"类别中的560万人主要反映IT和工程领域——不是因为印度缺少其他专业人员，而是因为那些是ILOSTAT能统计到的职业。与此同时，Claude使用数据（58,098次对话）几乎全部来自班加罗尔、海得拉巴和浦那的IT行业。结果是：印度的数据讲述了一个关于其科技行业的连贯故事，但对其90%的经济体本质上是沉默的。
  • 菲律宾：类似问题但规模较小。55个ISCO代码有非零实测暴露度（英国为144个）。BPO（业务流程外包）行业——呼叫中心、数据录入——主导了Claude使用，造成了偏颇的视角。菲律宾的4690万个岗位包括大量农业和非正规服务业部门，这些部门对ILOSTAT的细粒度数据和Claude使用模式都是不可见的。

7. 时间快照

V4数据覆盖单一周（2025年11月13-20日）。AI采用模式在快速演变。一家公司在采样周内部署Claude企业版可能显著扭曲小国的数据。理论暴露度（Anthropic任务分析）也是2026年初Claude能力的时间点快照。

This document contains structured data on 342 US occupations from the Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Outlook Handbook, each scored for AI exposure on a 0-10 scale by an LLM (Gemini Flash). Use this data to analyze, question, and discuss how AI will reshape the US labor market.

## Scoring methodology

Each occupation was scored on a single AI Exposure axis from 0 to 10, measuring how much AI will reshape that occupation. The score considers both direct automation (AI doing the work) and indirect effects (AI making workers so productive that fewer are needed).

A key heuristic: if the job can be done entirely from a home office on a computer — writing, coding, analyzing, communicating — then AI exposure is inherently high (7+), because AI capabilities in digital domains are advancing rapidly. Conversely, jobs requiring physical presence, manual skill, or real-time human interaction have a natural barrier.

Calibration anchors:
- 0-1 Minimal: roofers, janitors, construction laborers
- 2-3 Low: electricians, plumbers, firefighters, dental hygienists
- 4-5 Moderate: registered nurses, police officers, veterinarians
- 6-7 High: teachers, managers, accountants, journalists
- 8-9 Very high: software developers, graphic designers, translators, paralegals
- 10 Maximum: data entry clerks, telemarketers

## Scoring methodology

Each occupation was scored on a single AI Exposure axis from 0 to 10, measuring how much AI will reshape that occupation. The score considers both direct automation (AI doing the work) and indirect effects (AI making workers so productive that fewer are needed).

A key heuristic: if the job can be done entirely from a home office on a computer — writing, coding, analyzing, communicating — then AI exposure is inherently high (7+), because AI capabilities in digital domains are advancing rapidly. Conversely, jobs requiring physical presence, manual skill, or real-time human interaction have a natural barrier.
